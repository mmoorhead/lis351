Why the Vienna System is the Best 1.e4 Opening for Beginner Chess Players

The Vienna System, beginning with 1.e4 e5 2. Nc3, has slowly developed into my new favorite e4 weapon. It's variety, diversity of lines, and solid foundation make it a great weapon for beginners who want to deviate from typical Nf3 lines and spice things up.
These are typical lines that I recommend all beginners look into for solid, but tactical chess:

1. Vienna Gambit - 1. e4 e5 2. Nc3 Nf6 3. f4 exf4 4. e5 Ng8 5. d4.
Over 6000 lichess players have fell into this typical, 5 move trap that gets white a solid positional advantage that is hard to navigate for black.

2. Vienna Game: Copycat Variation - 1. e4 e5 2. Nc3 Nc6 3. Bc4 Bc5 4. Qg4 Qf6 (4... g6 5. Qf3 Nf6 6. Nge2) (4... Nf6 5. Qxg7 Rg8 6. Qxf7#) 5. Nd5 Qxf2+ 6. Kd1
In the best version, a classic 6 move mate, in the worst version, a very stable position that gives white a tempo and space advantage. I recommend looking deeper into the variations that don't include 4.Qf6.

3. Vienna Game: Mainline - 1. e4 e5 2. Nc3 Nc6 3. Bc4 Nf6 4. d3 Bc5 5. f4
A mainline-style Vienna that will teach beginners how to balance with an even position.
